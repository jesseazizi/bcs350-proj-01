## BCS 350 Questionnaire
<details>
<summary>Part 1: Content</summary>

1. What is the name of your business?
> FSCDrive.

2. Is the business real or fictional?
> Fictional.

3. What products or services does the business offer?
> Cloud storage + class integration for FSC students.

4. Who is the target audience?
> FSC students and instructors.

5. What problem does this business solve?
> Students often use different cloud storage systems (Google Drive, Microsoft OneDrive, etc.) for storing class-related documents. This creates issues, like when students want to share documents with one another to collaborate on projects simultaneously. Creating one platform for all students to use that automatically organizes their documents by semester and lets them create groups to work on projects together makes being a student much easier.

6. What makes this business unique compared to competitors?
> FSCDrive is a cloud storage platform optimized and tailored specifically to FSC students.

7. What pages will your website include?
> Home, About Us, Services, Contact.

8. Write a short mission statement for the business.
> "*We're in the business of making cloud storage easier and more accessible to all FSC students and instructors.*"

9. What tone should the website content have?
> Professional but also friendly.

10. What call-to-action do you want visitors to take?
> Contact support if they need help regarding FSCDrive.

11. What information must be immediately visible on the homepage?
> Everything in the navbar + mission statement and general description of the service.

12. Will the site include images, videos, or other media? Why?
> I will include some generic clipart but FSCDrive does not have a logo or other promotional materials so I cannot include such things.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Part 2: Content</summary>

1. What overall style will the website have (minimalist, corporate, creative, etc.)?
> Minimalist so it's easily legible.

2. What color scheme will you use and why?
> The same shade of green that represents FSC to reinforce the association with the school.

3. What fonts will you use for headings and body text?
> Arial since it's easily legible.

4. How will spacing and layout improve readability?
> Fonts and overall design are kept simple to maximize readibility. There will be lots of spacing between elements so the site doesn't feel cramped.

6. What visual hierarchy will guide the user’s attention?
> The navbar at the top will be used to navigate between the various different pages while everything below is the content of each page.

7. How will consistency be maintained across pages?
> Each page will use the same color scheme/fonts. The navbar will stay in the same position as well so the user can always refer back to it. Each page will have a heading indicating which page the user is currently on.

8. What icons will you use?
> I don't plan to use any icons. Instead the navbar words and page messages will be kept simple so it's easily to read the text on screen and recognize what represents what.

9. What emotions should the design evoke in users?
> Friendliness, usability, and the connection with FSC to reinforce the intersection between cloud storage and education.

10. How will accessibility be considered (contrast, font size, etc.)?
> The site's color scheme is high-contrast and the fonts are all sans-serif so they are easily legible. Additionally, any images will have alt text so users with screen-readers can understand them.

11. What inspiration sources influenced your design?
> The myFSC website and the Office 365 dashboard.

12. Describe the layout of the homepage and build the wireframe
> The homepage will have a navbar at the top, a welcome message, a large picture, and instructions for navigating the website. The wireframe can be found inside the repository.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
Part 3: Interactivity
</summary>

1. What interactive elements will your site include (forms, buttons, menus, etc.)?
> There will be clickable links along the navbar at the top and a form on the "Contact Us" page to submit a support ticket.

2. How will users navigate the website?
> Users can refer to the navbar at the top to switch between the website's various different pages.

3. Will there be form validation or user feedback? Explain.
> The entries on the navbar will change colors when hovered over so the user recognizes that they are clickable links. Additionally, the "Contact Us" page will let the user know when the ticket has been successfully submitted.

4. What JavaScript functionality will you implement?
> None as of yet, but there will be a script file if I change my mind.

5. How does interactivity improve user experience?
> The highlighted buttons help users understand that clickable elements can be interacted with.
